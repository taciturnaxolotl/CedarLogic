Stepping

1. CEDAR offers an advanced circuit simulation feature called stepping. To start the stepping portion of this tutorial, put a clock on the C input of your full adder circuit as shown.

2. Press the pause button on the toolbar. Notice how the simulation freezes and the pause button becomes depressed on the toolbar. Whenever the pause button is depressed, CEDAR's simulation is paused.

3. Now press the step key several times on the toolbar. Notice how the clocks output changes as you press the step button. The step button allows you to step forward one time step in the simulation. This feature can come in handy for debugging large circuits and observing signal propagation in circuits. Note that the step function can only be used when the circuit is paused.
